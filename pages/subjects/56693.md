<p>Was a trustee of the Cemetery from 1831-1833. Was one of the founding members of the Massachusetts Horticultural Society and served as their vice president from 1829-1834 and the president of MHS in 1835.  He was a successful businessmen in insurance in Boston. Cook spoke at the second annual festival of the Mass. Hort. Society in 1830. Buried in Lot 397 on Rose Path.</p>
